Consumer Discipline in Global Warming

The phrase - “Disciplined Customer”, is quite a huge term to talk about as there are multiple factors involved with this. But there are somethings in it which, if we are aware about and consider in our day-to-day life, can contribute to earth and make it a better place for human beings and other species. The world is moving too fast and so are we. Everyone wants fast solutions for everything. Especially when it comes to our daily needs we opt for fast foods, fast fashion, etc. But sadly while doing all these, we have side lined or ignored the most important things which are OUR OWN HEALTH and the PLANET HEALTH. To fulfil all these customer needs and demands and to survive in this competitive industrial cat-race, most of the manufacturing industries have adopted various methods (for raw material, manufacturing, waste disposal, etc.) and chemicals which are really harmful for the environment (water, air, soil) and eventually to the living beings. So the best way to alter this situation for better is by changing our requirements. Well I know we can’t revert it all overnight but we surely can contribute little bit by just increasing our awareness a bit as “End Users” of all these companies. the very basic things we might start doing are -

Stop single-use plastic right away - You are hearing this one since years. But have you started doing it? No?? Well then let’s start it now as Plastic pollution has afflicted land, waterways and oceans. Aborting use of plastic everywhere 100% is not possible but fortunately, we do have some amazing alternatives for single-use plastic like

Bottled water → Reusable water bottle & tap water
To go beverage → Travel mug
Straws and plastic cutlery  → Bamboo or other types of reusable cutlery, edible cutlery
Plastic bag → reusable bag
Balloons, including string/holder → Bubbles, kites, paper ribbons and streamers, flags, tissue balls
Sponges and dish clothes → Scrubbers and cloths made from natural fibres
Cleaning products → Bar soap, dissolvable cleaning pods
Toothpaste and mouthwash → Toothpaste/ mouthwash tablets
Soap, shampoo etc. → All exist in form of bars
Plastic cotton bud → Single-use cotton bud made from bamboo or paper or reusable cotton bud
Menstrual products (tampons, pads) → Menstrual cups, reusable pads, period underwear plastic-free products
Wet wipes, diapers → Washable cloth diapers, plastic- free and biodegradable diapers/ wet wipes

Chose environment friendly products - Have you ever noticed what your product contains while buying it? Every manufacturing company is bound to mention all the ingredients used for making of that product (especially food and beauty products); under product description so that the end user knows what he/she is buying; and it is our responsibility to see this information carefully and buy wisely to make sure that it does not include any harmful chemicals.

Know your manufacturer – While choosing any brand make sure to gather some really important information about them to assure product quality, safety, supply chain transparency, brand reputation, warranty and support. Overall, knowing the manufacturer of the product can help you make informed purchasing decisions and support ethical and sustainable buying practices.

Say bye to fast fashion – Unfortunately fast fashion has spoiled the planet so much and so fast that fashion industry is declared as one of the major cause for global warming. And it is our responsibility cum necessity to change this picture. For details read FAST FASHION - A Slow Curse

Overall, a customer’s mindset is first condition for healthy and a safe planet. So let’s make it a habit to choose wisely and become disciplined customer and a responsible citizen for our happier and healthier planet.

-Vaishali Udmale
